Pag-IBIG MP2 Calculation Guide

The Modified Pag-IBIG 2 (MP2) is a voluntary savings program with a 5-year maturity. It is one of the most popular investment vehicles in the Philippines because it historically earns a higher dividend rate than the regular Pag-IBIG savings, banks, and time deposits. Dividends are tax-free and government-guaranteed.

Computation Logic

Dividends are calculated based on your Average Monthly Balance (AMB) multiplied by the dividend rate declared for the year.

  • Year 1: Your monthly contributions accumulate. The AMB for new money effectively earns roughly half the annual rate because contributions made in December only earn for 1 month, while January contributions earn for 12 months.
  • Year 2-5: Your previous balance (Capital + Earned Dividends in Compounded Mode) earns the full annual rate since it sits in the fund for the entire year.

While past performance does not guarantee future returns, MP2 has consistently performed well.

Payout Options

Compounded (5-Year Maturity): Dividends are re-invested, allowing your money to grow faster through the power of compounding. You receive the total principal plus all dividends after 5 years. This is recommended for long-term wealth building.

Annual Payout: You receive the dividend earnings via bank/check every year. Your principal remains intact but does not grow from re-invested earnings. This is ideal if you need annual passive income.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Can I withdraw before 5 years?

Yes, but generally only for specific reasons (medical, unemployment) without penalty. Voluntary early withdrawal may result in receiving only 50% of earned dividends.

Is there a limit to how much I can save?

There is no maximum limit. However, for one-time remittances exceeding ₱500,000, you may need to present proof of income source.
